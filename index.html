Verified specifications

LingBot-Map

  • Architecture: Geometric Context Transformer (anchor context, pose-reference window, trajectory memory) [1]
  • Map output: dense 3D reconstruction [1][3]
  • Accuracy: 6.42 m ATE on Oxford Spires, ~2.8x better than prior streaming SOTA [5]
  • ETH3D F1: 98.98 [3]
  • Stability: 10,000+ frames streaming [1]
  • Compute: needs NVIDIA GPU (4.63 GB model) [2]
  • License: Apache 2.0 (commercial-friendly) [2]
  • Maturity: released Apr 2026, not yet flown on UAVs [3]

ORB-SLAM3

  • Architecture: feature-based visual / visual-inertial / multi-map SLAM [4]
  • Map output: sparse ORB feature point cloud [4]
  • Accuracy: 3.6 cm ATE on EuRoC drone (stereo-inertial) [4]
  • Speed: real-time at 30-40 FPS on CPU [4]
  • vs. prior methods: 2-10x more accurate [4]
  • Robustness: multi-map (Atlas) survives tracking loss / relocalizes [4]
  • Compute: CPU-capable, low footprint [4]
  • License: GPLv3 (copyleft - constrains commercial integration) [6]

Methodology & honesty note. The radar shows illustrative relative positioning under the conditions you select — a decision aid, not a head-to-head benchmark run, since the two systems are not benchmarked against each other on a common UAV dataset. All figures in the "Verified specifications" and stat cards above are sourced (see references). The two accuracy numbers are not directly comparable: ORB-SLAM3's 3.6 cm is on the indoor/short EuRoC drone benchmark; LingBot-Map's 6.42 m is on the large-scale outdoor Oxford Spires sequence — different scales and conditions. LingBot-Map is weeks old and not yet flight-validated on UAVs (its benchmarks are largely ground/handheld), which is exactly why our plan includes running the open-source model over real GPS-denied drone footage as live proof.
